Design Principles in the Wild

Holly Osmer - MI 349

1. Affordance

This screenshot contains an example of affordance. Affordances are the physical characteristics that influence the functionality of an object. The 3D button that says "Tweet" with a quill next to it, gives the impressing that when someone is ready to post what they have just typed out, they should "press" that button and it will send the tweet out.

2. Mapping

This is an example of mapping, from Google Maps. The layout is extremely easy to navigate. The symbols area easy to interpret and very well place. That goes for the bottom as well. If the user were to want ot find their location, view satelite view, etc, the bottom corners are always the first place to look. Their visible, but not in the way.

3. Chunking

This is an example of chunking, from Buzzfeed. Chunking is the combining of information into a limited amount of chunks for easier processing and recall. The layout of this landing page on Buzfeed is very well layed out. The basic information of each article is well-summed up through the headlines and few follow-up sentances.

4. Signal-to-Noise Ratio

Signal-to-Noise ratio is the ratio of relevant to irrelevant information in a display. This landing page is a good example to good signal-to-noise ratio because everything is very straightforward and easy to comprehend at first glance.

5. Hick's Law

Hick's Law explains the time it takes to make a decision increases as the number of alternatives increases. The layout of Apple's home page is so simple, and once again straightforward, most users come to the sight already knowing what they want, or not taking any time at all figuring it out. The navigation options are simple and the design is not overwhelimg.
